Samsung

Samsung transformed from a 1960s television manufacturer into the world's largest smartphone brand. By pioneering AMOLED displays and developing the Android-based Galaxy series, the company has driven mobile innovation for over two decades—from 1999’s first MP3 phone to today's advanced foldables.

Iphone

The iPhone was first announced on January 9, 2007, by Apple CEO Steve Jobs. Billed as a revolutionary "three-in-one" device, it launched on June 29, 2007, transforming the smartphone market forever by replacing physical keyboards with an intuitive multi-touch touchscreen and introducing the App Store.

Huawei

Huawei was founded in 1987 by Ren Zhengfei in Shenzhen, China, starting with just $5,000 in capital. Originally a reseller of imported private branch exchange (PBX) switches, the company evolved into a global telecommunications and consumer electronics powerhouse, overcoming strict U.S. trade sanctions to emerge as a leader in AI and hardware.

Google Pixel

The Google Pixel line debuted in October 2016, replacing the developer-focused Nexus series. Designed to showcase Google's vision of hardware and artificial intelligence (AI) integration, the lineup is renowned for its industry-leading computational photography and clean Android experience.

Tecno

Founded in 2006, Tecno Mobile is a smartphone brand established in Hong Kong by Chinese entrepreneur George Zhu. It operates as a subsidiary of Transsion Holdings, a Shenzhen-based technology group that designs and manufactures devices specifically tailored for emerging markets across the globe.

Infinix

Infinix Mobile is a Hong Kong-based smartphone brand founded in April 2013 by Transsion Holdings. Designed with a strong focus on the youth market, Infinix was created to deliver cutting-edge technology, fantastic smartphone experiences, and stylish design at highly competitive price points.
